With continuous recession of the world economy as well as development of the Internet and mobile technology, the second-hand e-commerce and the second-hand market shares have been constantly growing. The Chinese people living in Korea are mainly concentrated in the capital circle of Seoul, Gyeonggi and Incheon, the same regions as those with brisk second-hand transactions via Karrot. Karrot APP is the most popular smartphone application program among the second-hand transaction platforms in Korea. In this research, through investigations on second-hand transaction circumstances in the region, the author probes experience of the Chinese consumers living in Korea in second-hand transaction via Karrot.

There are two problems studied in this research. The first one is social and cultural significance that brisk second-hand transactions among people from China and Korea might generate on society of Korea. The second one is ways to develop and improve second-hand transaction culture among consumers from China and Korea. In this research, the author adopts qualitative research methods and applies in-depth interviews for data collection. There are four findings in this research. First, the Chinese users regard second-hand transactions as a window for buying goods. Second, the Chinese people in Korea can come into contact with the local culture through second-hand transactions. Third, the process of participation in transactions offers consumers interesting experience like playing games or role-play. Fourth, with continuous economic downturn, consumers that originally pursued cost performance turn to pursuing mental satisfaction with prices now. There are four contributions in this research. First, it shows how consumers’ values and behaviors give expression and conflict in terms of reality. Second, it explains cultural values of second-hand transaction consumption by people from China and Korea. Third, it explains second-hand transaction second-hand transaction of the Chinese and Korean consumers and optimizes transaction experience. Fourth, it provides reference for hyper-locality.
